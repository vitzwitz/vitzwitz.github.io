About Me

I am a python developer, a manager, and an aspiring numerical mathematician. I graduate Rochester Institute of Technology with a bachelor of science in applied mathematics. I am a member of the Structural Biology Extensible Visualization Scripting Language research team. I write machine learning concepts such as k-nearest neighbors and principal components analysis in efforts to update their plug-in into a stand-alone software. I am a manager at a coffee shop on campus since January of 2016. Math and programming are my passions, and I want to explore various fields throoughout my career such as data science, IT, software engineering, robotics, research, game design, and web and app development.

I started at Rochester Institute of Technology as a physics major, but two and a half years late, I realized that it was the wrong field for me. However, it helped me rediscover a passion I have had since elementary school: math. I had a couple friends in the math department, and they seemed well pleased with their studies, so I switched to applied mathematics in 2015. I took a few mathematical modeling courses, and my opinion on programming changed; I went from hating coding to loving it. Ever since, I try to learn as many languages as I can. I am always eager to learn more about computer science, numerical analysis, databases, model building, algorithms, and software engineering. By the end of Decemeber of 2016, I joined a biochemical research team to incorporate my mathematical knowledge to their plug-in. My work has introduced me to data science, and I have decided that this is the career for me. I realized that I want to develop and write algorithms, create and manage databases, learn about unfamiliar fields such as biochemistry, apply machine learning concepts to software, and program in the industry.
